Who I Am

I am a video game designer and writer who lives in Portland, Oregon, where I’m active in the indie game community. I spend my time trying to do “new stuff’ with narrative in my own games, and playing every game I can get my hands on to learn from them. I enjoy all aspects of the creative and production process; writing, bringing together creative and technical team members, and programming. I’m also an experienced freelance video editor. I graduated from Columbia College Chicago with a B.A. in Film.

I am currently studying computer science in Portland while working on my games.

Waiting For The Loop

Writing, programming, design, VO direction

A sci fi family drama visual novel in which a brilliant computer scientist builds an artificial intelligence that passes as human… but also attracts the attention of the government. Team of 6. Features characters and events from Throw Me In The River and Interstellar Borders, but can be enjoyed and understood without knowledge of either. Fully voice acted, with 450+ lines of dialog. Roughly 15 minutes long. There are multiple endings, and one secret ending. Made in one week fora game jam hosted in Portland, Oregon by Portland Indie Game Squad, then touched up over the course of two additional weeks.

Waiting For The Loop Trailer from Reis Mahnic on Vimeo.

Shifters

Solo Project

A story driven visual novel in which a paranoid scientist with a flair for the dramatic teaches a seminar on the existence of Shape Shifters who live among us. Solo Project. For this project, I was entirely on my own (I entered this game into the ‘Compo’ category for Ludum Dare). Shifters placed #91 in Mood, #118 in Theme and #122 in Humor in Ludum Dare 35.

Hot Cuppa Joe

Writing, design, voice acting

Help Joe Cuppa save his friends with his sick burns! Team of 5. Hot Cuppa Joe placed in the top 16 entries for the Cartoon Network Game Jam. All screenshots above are from builds of Hot Cuppa Joe during development, and were shared in blogs and social media while the game was in production. Hot Cuppa Joe is currently unavailable for download, but will be made available on Cartoon Network’s website in 2017.
